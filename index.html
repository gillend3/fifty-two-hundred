50-200

This app is based on a suggested writing exercise from George Saunders. The idea is to write a 200-word short
story (exactly 200—not a word more or less) using fifty unique words or less. When faced with these
constraints, particularly the inability to over-describe things, writers often respond by driving the narrative
forward (because, after all, what else is there to do!), which leads to rising action—the heart of a short
story.

Instructions: Start typing away on your short story. The app will keep track of each unique word for you and
present them in a list. When you’ve used fifty unique words, you won’t be able to introduce anymore.
You also won’t be able to type over two-hundred words. You can use the Copy button at the bottom
if you want to copy & paste your work into a word processor.
